Tower of Hanoi

Instructions

In 1883, French mathematician Édouard Lucas invented a puzzle based on a legend about Indian Monks.

To complete the puzzle, move the entire tower from one column to another, one piece at a time, while obeying the following rules:

  1. You may only pick up the top tile of a stack
  2. You may only hold one tile at a time
  3. You may only place a tile on another tile larger than itself

Click anywhere within one Column to select the top tile, and then click in another Column to place it.

On this website, you can play with between Three and Seven tiles. How many tiles would you like to play with?

The Legend of Hanoi

According to legend (which may or may not have been fabricated by Édouard Lucas himslef), at the Kashi Vishwanath Temple in India, there is with three poles, one of which is holds a tower of 64 golden discs. Acting out an ancient prophecy, the priests having been moving these discs in accordance with the above stated rules. When the puzzle is finally completed, it will bring about the end of the world!

If the priests were to move one disc per second and make the smallest number of moves possible, it would take them 585 Billion years to finish. Fortunately, that's a long, long way off.
